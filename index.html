Projects

Master’s Project: TRNG Post-Processing (2023 – 2025)

Designed a True Random Bit Generator (TRNG) based on a 1st-order 4-bit, Continuous-Time ΔΣ ADC in TSMC 65 nm technology. The design leverages thermal noise, jitter, and metastability sources to achieve 100 Mbps throughput with an energy efficiency of 183 pJ/bit.

Proposed and implemented a Barrel Shifter & XOR-chain based post-processing method, benchmarking it against DES, PRESENT, and AES cryptographic modules. To ensure industry-standard security, I implemented all 15 NIST SP 800-22 statistical tests in Python for automated randomness validation and p-value analysis.

Mixed-Signal ADC Designs

10-bit, 32 MS/s SAR ADC: Designed an asynchronous SAR ADC with a split CDAC and StrongARM comparator in GF SiGe BiCMOS 90 nm; achieved approximately 9.3 ENOB.

8-bit 2-Step Flash ADC: Developed a 200 MS/s subranging ADC in TSMC 65 nm CMOS using a flash front-end and R-2R DAC residue stage; achieved INL/DNL < 0.5 LSB with 90 mW power at 1.2 V.

12-bit Dual-Slope ADC: Designed a fully differential dual-slope ADC in TSMC 65 nm for improved noise immunity and inherent 60 Hz line-noise rejection, analyzing linearity through extracted transfer characteristics.

RF & Millimeter-Wave Circuits

2.4 GHz Class-AB Power Amplifier: Simulated a Class-AB CMOS PA in TSMC 65 nm featuring a cascode output stage; achieved ~16.5 dBm Pout and evaluated PAE and AM-AM/AM-PM distortion.

Analog, Digital & Power Management
