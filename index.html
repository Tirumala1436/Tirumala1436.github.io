Projects

Master’s Project: TRNG Post-Processing (2023 – 2025)

Developed a True Random Number Generator (TRNG) using a 1st-order 4-bit Continuous-Time ΔΣ ADC in TSMC 65nm technology, leveraging multiple entropy sources such as thermal noise, jitter, and circuit metastability.

The project focused on a novel Barrel Shifter & XOR-chain based post-processing method to enhance the statistical quality of the raw random bits. This post-processing approach was benchmarked against established cryptographic modules like DES, AES, and PRESENT ciphers, demonstrating superior effectiveness in boosting randomness quality and security while maintaining low energy consumption and high throughput.

The TRNG achieves a throughput of 100 Mbps and energy efficiency of 183 pJ/bit. Randomness validation was performed using all 15 NIST SP 800-22 statistical tests implemented in Python.

Bachelor’s Project: 8-bit 2-Step Flash ADC

Designed a 200 MS/s 8-bit ADC with subranging architecture achieving excellent linearity: INL/DNL < 0.5 LSB. The ADC consumes 90 mW power @ 1.2V and occupies an area of 0.30 mm2. Comprehensive post-layout validation confirmed manufacturability and performance.

Bachelor’s Project: 140 GHz CMOS Low Noise Amplifier (LNA)

Developed a 6-stage AC-coupled cascode LNA with 20% stage scaling. Demonstrated power consumption of 63 mW @ 1.2 V and compact layout (300 µm × 500 µm). Suitable for millimeter-wave wireless applications, such as 5G, radar, and imaging, which require high gain and low noise.
